# Data Center-Driven Congestion Shifts in PJM — Cloud XF Constraint in Southern Dominion (2026-04-07)

## Summary

A new constraint, **Cloud XF**, has begun binding in Southern Dominion, sending considerable congestion uplift to two data center load nodes in Southern Virginia. This is a new pattern as of April 2026, driven by the combination of transmission work, sink-side datacenter demand, and solar generation.

## Key Points

### Constraint Details
- **Constraint**: Cloud XF (Southern Dominion)
- **Began binding**: April 2026
- **Affected nodes**:
  - **EASTERS 230 KV LD5_Z3** — located at the Microsoft Boydton data center campus
  - **CLOUD 230 KV LD4Z25** — located next to an unknown data center due south of Easters
- Both are **load nodes** at data centers in Southern Virginia

### Drivers
- Transmission work in Southern Virginia
- Sink-side demand strength at data centers
- Solar generation (likely exacerbating flow patterns)

### Broader Context
- "Data center alley" already sees pockets of datacenter-driven congestion
- Shifting development trends and a push towards areas with **easier access to transmission** will trigger new constraints
- Existing systemic congestion will remain under pressure as load grows

## Trading Implications

- Cloud XF is a **new constraint** — not yet priced into historical congestion models or spreads. Monitor for persistence beyond the current transmission outage window.
- If this constraint persists after transmission work completes, it signals **structural congestion** from datacenter load, not transient outage-driven congestion.
- Southern Dominion congestion adds another dimension to the AD-West spread thesis — datacenter load isn't just tightening the west-east interface, it's creating **localized nodal congestion** within Dominion itself.
- Solar interaction is worth tracking — if solar generation is contributing to binding, the constraint may have a **time-of-day pattern** (midday solar push against local demand).
