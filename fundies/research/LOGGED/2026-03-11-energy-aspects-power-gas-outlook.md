# Energy Aspects — Power & Gas Outlook (2026-03-11)

## Summary

EA's weekly update covers power and gas fundamentals for the week of March 9. The gas side is loosening: end-October storage estimate rose to 3.85 tcf on resilient L48 production (+2.9 bcf/d y/y), with res-com demand cratering (-10.7 bcf/d w/w) as winter fades. On power, PJM data-centre load forecasts remain weighted to the low end (base case ~21 GW by 2031 vs PJM's higher outlook), outages are rising as generators take deferred maintenance in warmer weather, and M3 gas forwards keep most gas in merit against coal this spring. Geopolitical risk from the Iran conflict has rallied AGT peak-winter 2027 forwards and WTI, but domestic producers have not yet responded with increased rig activity.

## Key Points

**Data Centre / Load Growth**
- EA tracks 66.3 GW planned PJM data-centre nameplate (net of BTM, excluding withdrawn/doubtful)
- Base case discounts greenfield survival to 25%, removes no-date projects → ~21 GW by 2031
- High-planned case aligns with PJM's outlook; low case assumes only active sites complete
- PJM weather-normalised load growth: +2.9% in 2026, +3.2% in 2027 (ERCOT leads at 5-6%)
- Winter peak load growing faster than summer in PJM due to heating electrification + BTM solar
- PJM summer peak: 166.2 GW (2026), 171.4 GW (2027); winter peak: 145.7 GW (2026), 153.0 GW (2027)

**Gas Supply & Storage**
- L48 production: 110.9 bcf/d WTD, down 0.6 bcf/d w/w (Tuesday effect)
- Appalachia: 33.4 bcf/d flat w/w; NEPA sub-regional flows +0.2 bcf/d w/w
- Permian: 22.6 bcf/d WTD; Haynesville: 12.5 bcf/d, -0.5 bcf/d w/w (erratic)
- EIA storage: -40 bcf estimated withdrawal for 6 Mar report
- End-October storage estimate: 3.85 tcf (+18 bcf w/w loosening) on resilient production (+2.9 bcf/d y/y)

**Gas Demand**
- Gas-for-power: 30.4 bcf/d for week ending 13 Mar, -2.9 bcf/d w/w
- Res-com: crashing — March avg 26.6 bcf/d, -14.8 bcf/d m/m on HDD attrition
- LNG feedgas: 18.3 bcf/d WTD, +1.0 bcf/d w/w (Cameron return); 97% capacity utilisation in March
- Canadian imports: 2.9 bcf/d WTD, -1.4 bcf/d w/w; Mexico exports: 6.9 bcf/d, +0.2 bcf/d w/w

**Trade & Infrastructure**
- AGT FG-27 forwards rallied on Winter Storm Fern fuel inventory risk + Iran conflict (LNG/oil price lift)
- NEXUS Hanoverton maintenance: -0.60 bcf/d capacity (down to 1.56 bcf/d), Apr 6 – May 4
- GCX expansion delayed to Q3 26 → Permian production upside limited until H2 26

**PJM Fuel Switching & Outages**
- M3 forwards put most gas in merit against coal this spring despite slight NAPP coal price rise
- PJM outages rising — generators deferred Feb maintenance now taking outage in warmer March
- Gas share of thermal generation expected >70% through shoulder season
- April gas gen forecast: +4 GW y/y; coal gen: -6 GW y/y on lower HH prices

**Coal**
- Domestic stocks healthy; flat-to-slightly-lower production y/y forecast
- Iran conflict persistence could disrupt stable coal price outlook via European export call

**Positioning & Rigs**
- Net positioning rebounded >100k lots net long on geopolitical instability
- Gas rigs: 132 (-2 w/w, +32 y/y); Oil rigs: 411 (+4 w/w, -76 y/y)
- Haynesville +1 rig w/w; Appalachia -1 (Utica); oil producers not yet reacting to WTI rally

## Trading Implications

- **Gas storage loosening** — 3.85 tcf end-Oct is bearish for summer gas, supportive of gas-for-power dispatch
- **PJM outages entering spring ramp** — expect rising outage MW through April, watch for congestion volatility
- **NEXUS maintenance Apr 6-May 4** — Appalachian gas to Midwest constrained, could widen basis
- **Gas in merit vs coal** — M3 levels favor gas dispatch; >70% gas share through shoulder keeps coal on the margin
- **Iran/geopolitics** — WTI rally not yet translating to rig response; AGT winter forwards elevated, monitor for pass-through to PJM spark spreads
- **Data centre load still uncertain** — EA's low-end weighting means actual PJM load growth could surprise either direction
