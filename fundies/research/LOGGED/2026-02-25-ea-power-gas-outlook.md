# Energy Aspects — Power & Gas Outlook (2026-02-25)

## Summary

EA's weekly update covers power and gas fundamentals for the week of 23 February. Gas storage is loosening sharply — end-March estimate fell 138 bcf w/w to 1.78 tcf as mild weather expectations persistently loosen balances. L48 production declined 1.0 bcf/d w/w to 110.14 bcf/d as a Northeast blizzard restricted Appalachian volumes. Golden Pass pipeline flows reached 0.27 bcf/d, signaling imminent first LNG production. On power, the NECEC-driven Mass-Maine price dislocation will persist this summer, NYISO will be 1.2 GW net longer y/y this summer, and PJM weather-normalised load growth is forecast at 2.9% for 2026 with winter peak growing faster than summer.

## Key Points

**Gas Storage & Balances**
- Estimated 49 bcf withdrawal for Thursday's EIA report; HDDs 19% below 10-year normal
- End-March storage estimate loosened 138 bcf w/w to **1.78 tcf** on mild weather expectations
- Persistent mild weather in two-week forecast loosening balances

**Gas Production**
- L48 production: 110.14 bcf/d WTD, -1.0 bcf/d w/w on Northeast blizzard
- Appalachia: 32.83 bcf/d WTD, -0.80 bcf/d w/w; NEPA flows restricted by blizzard, near-term resurgence expected
- Permian: 22.1 bcf/d WTD, +0.1 bcf/d w/w; GCX expansion deferred to Q3 26 limiting near-term upside
- Haynesville: 12.0 bcf/d WTD, -0.3 bcf/d w/w; volatile, Q2 26 expected at 13.0 bcf/d (+0.7 y/y)

**Gas Demand**
- Gas-for-power: 33.6 bcf/d for week ending 27 Feb, +2.7 bcf/d w/w; 68% gas share (-2 ppts w/w)
- Res-com: 40.7 bcf/d, +5.4 bcf/d w/w on NE blizzard
- LNG feedgas: 19.0 bcf/d WTD, +0.3 bcf/d w/w
- **Golden Pass**: Pipeline flows reached 0.27 bcf/d (+0.14 d/d); FERC request to introduce gas to cold end filed 24 Feb — imminent first LNG production

**Trade**
- Canada net imports: 5.7 bcf/d WTD, +0.6 bcf/d w/w on colder weather and Emerson constraint lifted
- Mexico exports: 5.5 bcf/d WTD, -0.7 bcf/d w/w on lower offshore gulf trade

**Power — ISONE / NYISO**
- NECEC-driven Mass-Maine price dislocation will persist this summer at high loads
- NYISO 1.2 GW net longer y/y this summer, but largely in zones east of Central-East constraint through Zone J
- Western NYISO zones could tighten due to HQ exports

**Power — ERCOT**
- ERCOT procured 1.2 GW more reserves y/y this winter; nearly 4 GW shifted from ancillaries to energy at high prices
- Low battery SOC had non-spin participation fall to 0% in some intervals
- ERCOT confirmed 1.6 GW of generators deployed through reliability unit commitments

**Load Growth**
- PJM weather-normalised load growth: +2.9% in 2026, +3.2% in 2027
- Winter peak load growing faster than summer in PJM (heating electrification + BTM solar)
- PJM summer peak: 165.9 GW (2026), 170.1 GW (2027); winter peak: 145.6 GW (2026), 152.9 GW (2027)

**LNG Canada**
- LNG Canada utilisation skyrocketed since January outage return; on pace for 0.85 Mt this month (~1.6 bcf/d, 71% utilisation)
- Raised LNG Canada feedgas forecast to 1.49 bcf/d (+0.15 bcf/d vs prior), further upside risk

**Coal & Industrial**
- Physical coal prices up w/w on export market dynamics
- Steel capacity utilisation at five-month high of 77.8% (Nucor ramp-up)
- Refinery utilisation at 91% on strong YTD runs

**Positioning**
- CFTC net positioning fell 14.9k lots w/w to 132.0k lots net long
- Rig counts net unchanged w/w

## Trading Implications

- **Gas storage loosening fast** — 138 bcf w/w loosening is material; bearish for summer gas pricing
- **Golden Pass imminent** — new LNG demand source; watch for ramp speed and impact on Gulf Coast basis
- **NE blizzard temporary** — Appalachian production drop will reverse; near-term resurgence expected
- **PJM load growth consistent** — 2.9% weather-normalised for 2026 matches later EA updates; winter peak growing faster than summer
- **NECEC reshaping ISONE** — Mass-Maine dislocation structural; NYISO longer but concentrated east of Central-East
