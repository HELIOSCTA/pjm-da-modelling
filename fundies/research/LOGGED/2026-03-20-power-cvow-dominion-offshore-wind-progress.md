# Recharge News — Dominion CVOW On Track for First Power Despite Trump (2026-03-20)

## Summary

Dominion states they are on track for first power at the Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind (CVOW) project, the largest US offshore wind array. Satellite imagery from 3/19/2026 shows:

- **2 new turbines completed** since the stop-work order was lifted earlier in 2026
- **2 existing pilot turbines** already in place
- **3rd turbine being installed** by Charybdis, Dominion's Jones Act-compliant jack-up vessel
- Total: ~4-5 turbines operational or near-operational

The project is proceeding despite the broader Trump administration headwinds for offshore wind.

## Key Points

- **First power imminent** — Dominion publicly confirming timeline despite political uncertainty
- **Charybdis (Jones Act vessel) is operational and installing** — this was a key bottleneck risk; vessel availability confirmed
- **Stop-work order lifted earlier in 2026** — only 2 turbines completed since then, suggesting slow installation pace
- **CVOW full buildout: 2.6 GW** (176 turbines × ~15 MW each) — at current pace of ~2-3 turbines since restart, full completion is years away
- **Located off Virginia Beach** — feeds into Dominion zone, directly relevant to Dom congestion dynamics

## Trading Implications

- **Near-term: negligible.** 2-3 new turbines = ~40-50 MW incremental capacity. Not material to the 31 GW nuclear + 40 GW gas stack
- **Medium-term (2027-2028): worth tracking.** If installation pace accelerates, CVOW adds offshore wind into the Dom zone during evening hours when onshore wind is typically weakest. Offshore wind has a flatter generation profile than onshore — could help with the evening ramp that's been driving Dom congestion this week
- **Structural long-term bearish for Dom congestion** — 2.6 GW of generation in the load pocket reduces N-S flow requirements. But full buildout timeline is highly uncertain under current political environment
- **Political risk remains** — "despite Trump" in the headline. Further federal action could slow or halt the project
