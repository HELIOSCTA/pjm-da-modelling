# Carbon Pulse — Virginia Publishes Proposed RGGI Regulation (2026-04-09)

## Summary

Carbon Pulse reports Virginia's DEQ has published draft regulatory text for RGGI re-entry under House Bill 29 (HB 29), including proposed base CO2 allowance budgets from H2 2026 through 2031+. The regulation reinstates Virginia's participation following exit at end-2023 and the election of Gov. Abigail Spanberger (D). The draft is AG-certified and under governor review, with HB 29 directing the DEQ to complete all steps by May 21.

## Key Points

- Draft regulation published per **HB 29**, reinstating Virginia's RGGI participation
- Six-month control period: **Jul-Dec 2026**, allowance budget **~11.5 Mst CO2**
- Virginia plans to participate in **Q3 and Q4 RGGI auctions** this year
- Proposed base budgets (declining trajectory):
  - 2027: **22.1 MtCO2**
  - 2028: **21.3 MtCO2**
  - 2029: **20.4 MtCO2**
  - 2030: **19.6 MtCO2**
  - 2031+: **19.6 MtCO2** (unless modified by future programme review)
- Virginia 2023 emissions under RGGI: **~23.6 MtCO2** vs adjusted budget of **~22.2 MtCO2** (already short in final year)
- RGGI states committed to **Fourth Program Review** no later than 2028
- DEQ says alignment of Virginia's base budget with current RGGI participants is "pending" — secondary regulation before 2027 to align with Third Program Review
- Regulation exempt from APA comment period, but DEQ may open one
- Effective date "not yet determined" per Regulatory Townhall, but HB 29 directs completion by **May 21**

## Trading Implications

- **Forward budget trajectory is bearish for VA generators**: budgets decline ~11% from 22.1 Mt (2027) to 19.6 Mt (2030), while VA load growth (data centers) is pushing emissions higher — compliance tightens year-on-year
- **2023 was already short**: 23.6 Mt emissions vs 22.2 Mt adjusted budget — if load growth continues, the gap widens under declining budgets
- **Third Program Review alignment** could further tighten budgets for 2027+ before year-end secondary regulation
- **Q3/Q4 auction participation** means allowance demand hits the market this summer — watch RGGI auction clearing prices
- Reinforces EA's $3.69/MWh spark uplift thesis; this article provides the regulatory detail behind those numbers
