# Energy Aspects — NA Power Outlook (March 2026)

## Summary

EA's monthly NA Power Outlook leads with PJM summer sparks outrunning gas prices on Middle East war jitters. PJM-W NQ on-peak power forwards have risen 7% since the last Outlook while delivered gas prices are only 1% higher — the market is pricing in risk that the war could tighten global coal balances with impact on PJM summer. EA raised their NQ26 PJM-W spark forecast to $49.18/MWh on higher model load expectations, rising coal price risk, and congestion adding premium to West Hub. DA on-peak PJM-W sparks have realised at $34.74/MWh (+$16.01/MWh y/y) despite gas being $1.08/MMBtu lower y/y, driven by higher load, increased congestion from transmission outages, and nuclear outages at Hope Creek and Calvert Cliffs. Peak-summer fossil call was recalibrated upward to 73.7 GW. Data centre load forecasts remain weighted toward the low end (~21 GW by 2031). Path risk for ISONE prompt winter sparks flagged as extremely high given uncertain war duration.

## Key Points

**PJM Sparks & Pricing**
- PJM-W NQ on-peak spark forwards up 7% since last Outlook; delivered gas only +1%
- Raised NQ26 PJM-W spark forecast to **$49.18/MWh** (from prior)
- DA on-peak PJM-W sparks realised $34.74/MWh (+$16.01/MWh y/y) over last two weeks
- Gas prices $1.08/MMBtu lower y/y but sparks much higher — load, congestion, nuclear outages driving the wedge
- Market worried Iran war could tighten global coal balances → PJM summer price risk

**Nuclear Outages & Congestion**
- Hope Creek and Calvert Cliffs on outage through mid-March
- Outages exacerbating congestion patterns around BGE and Northern Dominion
- Nuclear outages higher y/y since end of Winter Storm Fern
- Energy and congestion components of LMPs both rising y/y
- Load 0.6 GW higher y/y over last 14 days; renewables and nukes 1.1 GW and 0.5 GW lower respectively

**Fossil Call & Generation Mix**
- Peak-summer fossil call recalibrated from 73.2 GW to **73.7 GW** on higher weather-normal model load
- 2026 fossil call now higher than 2025 realisation
- Bridge: +2.7 GW load growth, -0.6 GW solar, -0.3 GW wind, -0.2 GW hydro, partially offset by +0.1 GW nuclear
- Gas share >70% through shoulder season on low gas prices

**Load Growth & Data Centres**
- AEP and Dominion zones dominate PJM load growth; ComEd and APS showing early signs
- 0.6 GW of hyperscale data centres tracked for Q1 26 connection
- Data centre load raising Dominion zone congestion; West Hub and AD Hub largely unchanged
- CVOW commissioning could flatten Dominion congestion trend from Q3 26
- 66.3 GW tracked PJM data centre nameplate (net of BTM, excluding withdrawn/doubtful)
- Base case: ~21 GW by 2031 (discounts greenfield survival to 25%, removes no-date projects)
- High-planned case aligns with PJM's outlook; low case assumes only active sites complete

**BTM / Co-Location**
- 758 MW of under-construction BTM power plants in PJM footprint
- AEP fuel cells, Homer City, Bluegrass Power likely to start construction this year
- PJM's proposed co-location solution: generators >50 MW must go through queue; fully islanded projects avoid queue but forgo grid service benefits

**Coal & Geopolitics**
- JV26 Henry Hub forwards +10% since start of Iran war; coal prices only slightly higher
- EA forecasts 45 Mt thermal coal exports in 2026 (+5 Mt y/y)
- Conflict lasting into April (not base case) could push coal higher
- European coal generation sees little upside absent a long war, limiting call on US tonnage

**Other ISOs (brief)**
- ERCOT-N on-peak summer sparks trading sideways above EA's $65/MWh forecast; limited geopolitical exposure
- ERCOT 7x8 sparks gaining over 5x16, particularly winter-spring — first time winter on-peak fossil call surpassed by off-peak
- CAISO: deeply negative prices returned late February; Sunzia flows restarted 6 March
- ISONE: Mass Hub NQ peak sparks ~$44/MWh vs EA forecast $18/MWh lower; NECEC delivering 1.1 GW as expected
- NYISO: CHPE COD expected 1 June, HQ's ICAP participation confirmed; bearish NYC capacity prices

## Trading Implications

- **PJM summer sparks rally has legs** — not just gas; coal price risk + congestion premium + higher fossil call all support. $49.18/MWh NQ26 spark forecast is above current settle
- **Nuclear outages = near-term congestion catalyst** — Hope Creek and Calvert Cliffs outages drive BGE/N.Dom congestion; watch for return-to-service dates as bearish catalyst
- **Dominion congestion structurally rising** — data centre load concentrated in AEP/Dominion; CVOW commissioning (Q3 26) is the key offset to watch
- **Coal is the sleeper risk** — Iran war extending into April could push global coal prices, directly lifting PJM summer given coal on the margin; EA doesn't have this as base case but flags it
- **Fossil call raised to 73.7 GW** — higher than 2025 realisation; bullish for summer peak pricing
- **BTM/co-location regulatory overhang** — PJM's proposed 50 MW threshold creates winners (islanded projects) and losers (grid-connected); watch for queue impact
