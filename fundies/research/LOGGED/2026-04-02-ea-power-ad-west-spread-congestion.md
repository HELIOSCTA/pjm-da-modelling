# Energy Aspects — NQ26 AD-West Spread: Dominion Load Supports Interface Utilisation (2026-04-02)

## Summary

EA forecasts fair value for the NQ26 DA on-peak AD-West spread at **-$10/MWh** (-$2.21/MWh y/y), vs the current futures price of **-$14.85/MWh**. The strip has widened $5/MWh YTD driven by record winter realisations. EA sees the spread narrowing from current levels but still wider y/y on ~1 GW Dominion load growth increasing west-east transmission interface utilisation. Limited transmission expansion before 2029 and AEP's long capacity position will sustain prevailing negative AD Hub congestion patterns.

## Key Points

### Spread View
- EA fair value: **-$10/MWh** for NQ26 DA on-peak AD-West
- Current futures: **-$14.85/MWh** — EA thinks this overshoots due to strong winter realisations carrying into spring
- YTD widening of **$5/MWh** driven by Winter Storm Fern and subsequent transmission outages
- FG26 on-peak AD-West realisation: record **-$38.06/MWh**

### Winter Record Congestion
- AD Hub DA on-peak congestion hit record low of **-$16.28/MWh** over FG26
- West Hub congestion reached record high of **$14.26/MWh**
- Dominion hit all-time load record of **25.2 GW** on 9 February
- Triple-digit Transco Zone 5 gas prices during the cold snap
- PEPCO nodes drove **>$15/MWh** DA congestion uplift at West Hub over peak winter
- PENELEC nodes congested down (long capacity, supply side of constraints)

### Dominion Load Growth & Interface Dynamics
- ~**1 GW** Dominion load growth y/y expected this summer
- More than a third of load growth in AEP (first season where AEP weather-normalised load growth outpaced Dominion)
- AEP dispatchable ICAP exceeded last summer peak load by **~15 GW** — massively long capacity but energy can't reach DOM due to interface limits
- Three key west-east interfaces: **AEP-DOM, AP-South, Bedington-Black Oak**
- On-peak interface utilisation averages **1.4 GW lower** in peak summer vs peak winter (solar offset)
- Virginia requires **1.1 GW fewer** on-peak net imports in summer vs winter

### Solar Offset (Partial)
- Virginia solar capacity up **0.3 GW** since last summer
- Another **0.4 GW** under construction with in-service before peak summer
- Solar offsets midday call on western imports but insufficient to reverse congestion trend
- Higher solar capacity factors reduce summer interface utilisation vs winter

### Transmission Expansion
- PJM approved **$6.3 billion** in bulk power transmission upgrades for west region / AEP load growth deliverability
- Only **25%** of western PJM RTEP projects have required in-service by 2027
- Most remainder entering operations **beyond 2028**
- Ratepayer advocates pushing back — claim data centre load should solely fund upgrades
- Limited west-east expansion over next 2 years will sustain/widen prevailing spread patterns

### Structural Congestion Trend
- AD Hub congestion as share of DA on-peak LMP has trended lower since flipping negative on 12-month trailing average in **mid-2024**
- Dominion congestion share more than **doubled** over the same period

## Trading Implications

- **AD-West spread**: EA sees -$14.85 as too wide — fair value -$10/MWh. Potential trade: buy AD-West (narrower) if you agree the winter carry-over is overdone. Still wider y/y though.
- **DOM congestion**: Structurally bullish — load growth, limited tx expansion, and capacity shortfalls in Mid-Atlantic/DOM vs AEP's long position
- **Solar hedge timing**: Solar additions reduce midday congestion but don't eliminate it — peak congestion hours may shift to morning/evening shoulders
- **RTEP timeline risk**: If tx projects slip further (ratepayer pushback), congestion could widen beyond EA's forecast — upside risk to the negative AD-West spread in 2027-2028
- **AEP basis**: Negative congestion at AEP/PENELEC nodes is structural given massive excess capacity — bearish AEP basis vs RTO
