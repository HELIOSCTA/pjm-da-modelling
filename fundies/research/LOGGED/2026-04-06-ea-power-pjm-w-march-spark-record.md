# Energy Aspects — PJM-W March On-Peak Sparks Hit Record on Higher Fossil Call, Transmission Outages (2026-04-06)

## Summary

PJM-W March DA on-peak sparks realised over **$39/MWh**, surpassing the decadal record set last March by nearly **$13/MWh**, despite **$0.87/MMBtu lower** delivered M3 prices. The outperformance was driven by a **0.7 GW higher fossil call y/y** and significant transmission outages creating congestion uplift.

## Key Points

### Spark Realisation
- March DA on-peak sparks: **>$39/MWh** (new decadal record)
- Prior March record exceeded by ~**$13/MWh**
- Achieved despite lower gas input costs (M3 -$0.87/MMBtu y/y)
- Fossil call **+0.7 GW y/y** — load growth absorbing lower gas prices

### Congestion / Transmission Outages
- Key transmission facility outages drove **upwards of $5/MWh y/y increase** in West Hub DA LMPs
- **Nottingham constraint** (PECO) binding through much of March → significant **North–South congestion** and uplift in **BGE and PEPCO**
- **Susquehanna Unit 1** (PPL; 1.3 GW) on outage from **23 March** → exacerbated constraints around **Conastone**
- Weather-normalised load growth of **2.9 GW PJM-wide** compounding congestion impacts

### April Outlook
- Transmission outages at **Graceton** beginning this month
- Could result in **similar congestion patterns** (North–South, BGE/PEPCO uplift)

## Trading Implications

- Spark strength is **structural, not gas-driven** — lower gas didn't compress sparks, higher fossil call and congestion dominated. This supports the thesis that PJM-W sparks have shifted to a higher regime.
- **Nottingham / Conastone** constraints are the mechanism — watch for continuation via Graceton outages in April.
- Susquehanna 1 outage (1.3 GW nuclear) is a material swing factor for Eastern PJM congestion; monitor return-to-service timeline.
- Validates EA's earlier call that transmission constraints, not gas, are the primary spark driver for NQ26 (see 2026-03-16 spark overvaluation note and 2026-04-02 AD-West spread note).
