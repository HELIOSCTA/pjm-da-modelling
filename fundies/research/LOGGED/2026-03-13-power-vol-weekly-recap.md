# Trident OTC — PJM Power Vol Weekly Recap (Week Ending Mar 6, 2026)

## Summary

Trident OTC's power vol desk notes a week of gradual but consistent repricing of upside risk in PJM. The market is embedding a persistent risk premium tied to the Iran siege and broader Middle East energy infrastructure risk. ATM implied volatility and 25d call skew firmed across all liquid tenors. NQ26 is the most extended part of the surface — ATM implieds above historical averages, skew above historical bounds, and a ~25 vol gap between ATM IV and realised volatility. Friday saw a notable gas-power divergence: gas forwards weakened with softer implieds, while power forwards remained firm on strong cash pricing. The desk flags Super El Niño risk as a potential driver for a significantly warmer summer and record-challenging 2027 temperatures.

## Key Points

**Surface Moves**
- ATM implieds and 25d call skew firmed across all liquid tenors
- **J26 ATM IV**: Rose 10.4% w/w to finish at **60.00%**; only 5.5 vols above 30-day realised vol — risk premium not overstretched
- **K26 25d risk reversal**: Moved from 4.4% to 5.8% w/w; sharp move but only modestly above historical average, well below upper bound (~9%)
- **NQ26**: Most extended area of the surface — ATM IV above historical averages, skew above historical bounds; ~25 vol gap to realised vol
- NQ26 25d call increased 1.45 vols relative to ATM w/w

**Swaptions**
- C27 ATM IV: Rose 0.85 vols w/w to 22.75%; in lower quartile historically (with 2022), roughly at average (ex-2022); room to move higher
- C27 25d RR: 4.50% (+0.25 w/w); ~1.5 vols below C28
- C28 25d RR: 6.00% (+0.30 w/w); **above historical upper bound**
- C27 7-day realised vol at 21.9%, only 0.8 vols below ATM implied

**Flow**
- Call buying consistent throughout the week
- **K26 75 call** particularly well bid; healthy upside demand across monthly tenors
- **NQ26**: Lack of natural sellers repeatedly forcing implieds higher; market searching for equilibrium
- NQ 125/75 fence traded for >22 points of skew — continued demand for upside structures
- Swaption prints: 85/100 1x2 call spread and 60/55 1x2 put spread reinforced steepening skew theme

**Relative Value**
- J and K realised vols above historical averages; J approaching historical upper bound
- M26 30-day realised vol roughly at historical average
- Q2 tenors: 30-day RV above 7-day RV
- NQ tenors: 7-day RV slightly above 30-day RV (reversal)
- NQ ATM IV vs realised gap ~25 vols — substantial

**Gas-Power Divergence (Friday)**
- Gas forwards weakened, softer implieds, flattening call skew
- Power forwards remained firm, supported by strong cash; ATM IV firming, call skew steepening
- Cross-commodity divergence is a notable development

**Desk View**
- Iran siege appearing medium-to-longer-term; drone warfare poses persistent threat to regional energy — upside tail risk unlikely to dissipate near-term
- **Super El Niño** risk: NOAA Ensemble and ECMWF models pointing toward stronger El Niño developing; could bring significantly warmer summer and challenge 2027 temperature records
- Early and projections could shift through spring, but worth monitoring

## Trading Implications

- **NQ26 is the pressure point** — most extended vol surface with skew above historical bounds and no natural sellers; any catalyst (geopolitical de-escalation, weather disappointment) could trigger a sharp unwind
- **Gas-power divergence supports EA's overvaluation thesis** — gas weakening while power holds = risk premium, not fundamentals; aligns with the Mar 16 EA piece showing M3 rising in tandem with fixed gas
- **K26 75 call demand = market positioning for summer upside** — consistent with elevated CFTC OI noted in EA's Mar 16 spark overvaluation piece
- **C28 skew above historical bounds** — longer-dated market is pricing in structural upside risk (data centre load? coal? climate?); this is new information beyond what EA covers
- **Super El Niño is a wild card** — if it materialises, the NQ26 risk premium and record-challenging spark levels could actually be justified; watch NOAA/ECMWF updates through spring
- **J26 vol relatively fair** — ATM IV only 5.5 vols above realised; less stretched than NQ26
