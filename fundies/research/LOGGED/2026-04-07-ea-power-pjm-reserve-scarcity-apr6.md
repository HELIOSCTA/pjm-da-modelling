# Energy Aspects — PJM Reserve Scarcity on April 6: Steep Net Load Ramp Amid Record Outages (2026-04-07)

## Summary

PJM experienced four five-minute intervals of **primary and synchronized reserve shortages** on April 6, producing a maximum **$1,700/MWh scarcity adder** in one interval and lifting system-wide RT prices to nearly **$3,300/MWh**. Total generator outages reached **59.1 GW** (highest YTD), while net load ramped **~14 GW between 18:00 and 20:00** as solar and wind generation fell. Spring 2026 has seen more frequent and deeper reserve shortages than spring 2025, and PJM's proposed reserve reform is unlikely before winter 2027.

## Key Points

### April 6 Event
- **Reserve shortages**: 4 five-minute intervals of primary + synch reserve shortage
- **Max scarcity adder**: $1,700/MWh in one interval
- **RT price**: ~$3,300/MWh system-wide at peak
- **Generator outages**: 59.1 GW — highest level YTD
- **Net load ramp**: ~14 GW between 18:00 and 20:00 (solar/wind drop-off)
- **DA forecast**: Above actuals at HE20, but **actual load exceeded forecast in HE21**

### Spring 2026 vs Spring 2025
- 2026 has seen **more frequent and deeper reserve shortages** to date
- Significant shortages on **12–13 March** (documented in your RT price cap TODO — $786 DART on 3/12, $1,363 spike on 3/13)
- Spring 2025 by comparison: only **one major scarcity event** by this date (8 April 2025, cool weather, ~$3,600/MWh for several intervals)

### Reserve Reform Timeline
- PJM's proposed **Ramp/Uncertainty Reserve** service (RCSTF) would address rising net load ramp uncertainty
- **Stakeholder approval uncertain**
- EA expects **earliest implementation: winter 2027**
- Current reserve design persists through all of NQ26

## Trading Implications

- **Scarcity is structural, not episodic** — spring 2026 is running hotter than spring 2025 on reserve shortages. The combination of 59 GW outages + 14 GW evening ramp + declining wind/solar is a repeating pattern, not a one-off.
- **$1,700/MWh scarcity adder confirms the ACE hypothesis** — your area-controlled-error TODO documented persistent negative ACE preceding this exact event. ACE went negative over the evening peak as generation failed to ramp ahead of net load.
- **DA forecast mismatch is asymmetric** — DA over-forecast at HE20 but under-forecast at HE21. The load surprise came one hour after the net load peak, during the sustained ramp. This is consistent with your operator-effect-on-congestion research (DA over-commits at peak, under-commits on the tail).
- **Reserve reform delay to winter 2027 is bearish for reform, bullish for scarcity** — current pricing design persists through summer 2026 and NQ26. No new ramp/uncertainty reserve product to absorb these events. EA's +$5-8/MWh LMP impact estimate from RCSTF won't materialize until at least 2027.
- **59.1 GW outages in early April** — and EA's spring outage forecast called for peak outages of 70 GW in late April. If that materializes alongside similar evening ramp conditions, expect more frequent shortage pricing through the month.
