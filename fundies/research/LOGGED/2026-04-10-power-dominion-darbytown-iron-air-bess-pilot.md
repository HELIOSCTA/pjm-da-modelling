# Dominion BESS-4 Pilot — Form Energy Iron-Air + Eos Zinc-Hybrid at Darbytown (2026-04-10)

## Summary

Dominion proposed a non-lithium-ion storage pilot at **Darbytown Power Station** (east of Richmond) in fall 2023, combining **Form Energy iron-air** (4.95 MW / 494 MWh) and **Eos Energy zinc-hybrid** (4 MW / 16 MWh) batteries. The project has a publicly stated cost of **$70.6M** and an original in-service date of **8/1/2026**. SCC approved the CPCN on 5/6/2024. Satellite imagery shows construction underway, though it's unclear which system is being installed first.

## Key Points

### Project Specs
- **Location**: Darbytown Power Station, Henrico County, east of Richmond
- **Form Energy**: 4.95 MW / 494 MWh iron-air (~100-hour duration)
- **Eos Energy**: 4 MW / 16 MWh zinc-hybrid (~4-hour duration)
- **Total cost**: $70.6M
- **EPC**: Sundt (selected late 2023)
- **CPCN**: Approved by Virginia SCC on 5/6/2024

### Cost Analysis
The $70.6M covers both systems, making per-technology cost allocation uncertain:

| Scenario | Form $/kWh | Eos $/kWh | Form Cost Share |
|----------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Blended (total project) | $138.4/kWh | — | — |
| 50/50 split | $71/kWh | $2,206/kWh | 50% |
| Form at target ($20/kWh) | $20/kWh | $3,485/kWh | 21% |
| Form at Google deal est. | ~$30/kWh | ~$2,800/kWh | ~25% |
| Eos at target ($160/kWh) | $138/kWh | $160/kWh | 96.4% |

- **Form Energy target**: <$20/kWh (Google deal suggests closer to $30/kWh)
- **Eos Energy target**: $160/kWh
- True cost breakdown likely somewhere between Form at 21-25% of total project cost

### Project Timeline
- **Fall 2023**: Dominion filed proposal
- **Late 2023**: Sundt selected as EPC
- **5/6/2024**: SCC CPCN approved
- **8/1/2026**: Original in-service date
- **Jan 2026**: Still listed as "under development" in Dominion's regulated generation portfolio
- **Current**: Satellite imagery shows graded surface and box-like containers beginning installation on western end

### Construction Status
- Google Earth imagery shows construction through 2025
- Newly graded and finished surface visible
- Box-like containers beginning installation on western end
- Unclear whether this is Form or Eos portion, or general site prep
- Henrico County describes it as "energy storage facility as an accessory use to the existing Dominion Energy Darbytown power station"
- If Form's portion, this would be the most advanced Form Energy project besides the Great River Energy pilot

## Trading Implications

- **Not a near-term price mover** — 4.95 MW / 494 MWh is tiny relative to PJM's 150+ GW system. This is a pilot, not a commercial deployment.
- **Long-duration storage is the long-term structural story** — if iron-air works at $20-30/kWh and scales, 100-hour duration batteries could fundamentally change overnight/shoulder pricing by storing cheap midday solar and discharging through multi-day low-wind periods.
- **Sited at Darbytown (Richmond), not in NoVA** — this doesn't directly address the data center corridor congestion. But Dominion's interest in non-lithium storage signals they're thinking about grid-scale duration beyond 4-hour Li-ion.
- **Evening ramp scarcity relevance** — the 4/6 event ($3,300/MWh on 14 GW net load ramp) is exactly the use case for long-duration storage. But at current pilot scale, this is years away from being commercially meaningful.
- **Watch for Dominion's next IRP** — if BESS-4 performs well, expect larger iron-air procurement in Dominion's next resource plan. That would be a longer-dated signal for PJM capacity and energy prices.
