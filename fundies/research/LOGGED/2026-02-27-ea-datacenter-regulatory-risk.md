# EA Insight — Regulatory Risk for PJM & ERCOT Data Centre Projects

## Summary

EA reports that tracked US operational data centre rated capacity rose 11.2 GW y/y, led by PJM (+3.7 GW) and ERCOT (+2.2 GW). However, regulatory hurdles in both markets pose significant uncertainty. In PJM, concurrent reforms around co-location rules, capacity market, and interconnection create policy uncertainty — the 50 MW co-location threshold, Connect and Manage curtailment risk, and BYONG pathway all affect developer economics. Dominion connected just 0.7 GW across 11 data centres in 2025 (lowest since 2021), reflecting lagged energisation. Construction activity is shifting to WECC, SERC, MISO and SPP (~17 GW under construction). In ERCOT, the LLIS-to-batch-study transition has created process delays even for late-stage projects.

## Key Points

**US Data Centre Capacity**
- Operational rated capacity +11.2 GW y/y since January 2025
- Led by PJM (+3.7 GW) and ERCOT (+2.2 GW)
- Data centre energisation accounted for over half of 11.9 GW Lower 48 weather-normalised load growth in 2025
- Nearly 50% of tracked under-construction capacity remains in PJM and ERCOT
- Construction rates rising in other markets: **~17.0 GW under construction** across WECC, SERC, MISO and SPP

**PJM Load Growth**
- PJM weather-normalised load grew ~2.2 GW y/y in 2025, led by Dominion zone at 1.0 GW y/y
- **Dominion**: Connected 0.7 GW across 11 DCs in 2025 (down 0.2 GW y/y, lowest since 2021) — reflects lagged energisation from prior additions
- Dominion expects 13 DC connections in 2026; incremental transmission upgrades completing spring through 2028
- Nearly 10.2 GW of Dominion's 48.5 GW large load queue has signed ESAs; new ESA signers face exit fees + higher collateral from Jan 2027
- **AEP**: Second-highest load growth at 0.9 GW y/y in 2025; contracted DC capacity now 11 GW OH, 3 GW IN, 1 GW VA
- Rapid AI DC construction in AEP, ATSI, PPL adds **0.5 GW upside risk** to EA's 2026 PJM growth forecast of 2.7 GW y/y
- **ComEd**: 0.7 GW DCs under construction (largest besides DOM and AEP); Exelon added ~0.2 GW DC demand in 2025

**PJM Regulatory Hurdles**
- **Co-location**: PJM filed FERC proposal amending co-location rules; 50 MW threshold for BTM generators — below threshold no interconnection agreement needed, but most DCs exceed it
- **BYONG (Bring Your Own New Generation)**: Co-locating DCs under BYONG avoid future capacity auction obligations and Reliability Backstop Auction
- **Connect and Manage**: Would force large loads to accept involuntary curtailment during emergencies — DC stakeholders largely oppose
- Multiple concurrent reforms creating significant policy uncertainty for developers

**ERCOT Load Growth**
- Weather-normalised load grew ~3.0 GW y/y in 2025 (ex-battery demand)
- Similar growth expected in 2026 but regulatory uncertainty limits grid-facing large-load energisation
- Oncor: 255 GW DC requests but only 38 GW meets SB 6 standards
- ERCOT close to approving 4 GW large-load transmission project in South Dallas (Dec 2028 target)
- AEP (ERCOT): 69 GW large-load queue, 36 GW contracted and SB 6 compliant
- CenterPoint: 2.5 GW under construction + 5 GW firm commitments in Houston; only 1.5 GW tracked DCs in Coastal zone

**ERCOT Regulatory Hurdles**
- LLIS fully implemented 15 Dec 2025, then replaced by new batch-study process days later
- Stakeholders report delays even for late-stage projects amid process uncertainty
- PUC Texas declined ERCOT fast-track of batch study to ensure stakeholder discussion
- First batch-0 large loads notified of qualification Q1 27; transmission plan to board Sep 2027
- Projects stuck in restudy loops in current process

## Trading Implications

- **PJM load growth may disappoint near-term** — regulatory uncertainty around co-location, Connect and Manage, and BYONG could slow DC energisation; DOM connected lowest since 2021
- **Construction shifting away from PJM/ERCOT** — 17 GW under construction in WECC/SERC/MISO/SPP signals diversification of DC demand away from congested markets
- **AEP is the upside risk** — 11 GW contracted in Ohio alone + rapid AI DC construction; 0.5 GW upside to EA's 2.7 GW PJM growth forecast
- **Dominion transmission upgrades key** — spring-through-2028 completion unlocks incremental demand; watch for completion timing vs ESA exit fee cliff (Jan 2027)
- **ERCOT grid-facing DC energisation limited through 2026** — batch study process won't yield results until Q1 27 at earliest
- **Co-location 50 MW threshold creates two tiers** — smaller projects avoid queue (bullish for near-term energisation), larger projects face queue delays (bearish)
