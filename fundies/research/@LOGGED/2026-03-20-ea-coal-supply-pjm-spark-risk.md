# Energy Aspects — US Coal Supply Risk & PJM Spark Implications (2026-03-20)

## Summary

Email exchange between Edi Lacic (Helios CTA) and Mike Lawn (Energy Aspects) on 2026-03-18 through 2026-03-20 covering Q2/NQ PJM spark spreads, coal market risk, and the current PJM fundamental setup.

**Thread 1 (3/18):** Edi asks if EA's view on Q2/NQ sparks has changed given recent PJM strength and strong load growth. Mike responds that load growth is in-line with EA expectations, but three factors are pushing sparks higher: (1) outages are looking "really strong" this year, (2) the energy component of LMP is "remarkably high for the load" suggesting outages are pushing up dispatched heat rates, (3) West Hub congestion is adding to realizations and transmission outages are "set to continue through early summer." EA is looking at revising their view daily. Edi adds that January voluntary load shedding may be masking/skewing load downward, and that Graceton outages "appear to be sticky until July."

**Thread 2 (3/20):** Edi asks about chatter that US/Eastern coal markets could resemble the 2022 event. Mike's key points: US inventories are healthier than 2022 start, but PJM inventories are "pretty low post-Fern" — any external draw on US coal could lift traded prices rapidly. US production is up y/y and supply chain is in better shape than post-COVID 2022. EA already raised US export forecast by 5m tons on Indonesia quota cuts, with headroom for another 5-6m tons if prices incentivize. Europe's coal upside is limited (~5 GW German reserve, staffing constraints). Asia has capacity to burn more coal already. Colombia is the marginal Atlantic basin source, Indonesia the marginal Pacific source. **EA has raised their summer spark forecast partly to account for coal price risk,** though prices haven't risen much yet. US coal industry is better positioned to respond with production than in 2022.

## Key Points

- **PJM coal inventories "pretty low right now post-Fern"** — vulnerable to external demand shock
- **EA raised summer PJM spark forecast** — partly on coal price risk from Indonesia quota cuts
- **Outages this year "really strong"** — pushing up energy component of LMP beyond what load alone would justify
- **Energy component of LMP "remarkably high for the load"** — transmission + generation outages are raising dispatched heat rates
- **West Hub congestion adding to realizations** — transmission outages "set to continue through early summer"
- **Graceton outages "sticky until July"** per Edi — confirmed by our 06d data showing Graceton-Manor returning then going back out 3/23
- **January voluntary load shedding may be masking true load growth** — if that load comes back, the load growth story is even more bullish
- **US coal production up y/y** and supply chain better than 2022 — limits the severity of a coal price shock vs the 2022 analog
- **Indonesia quota cuts** already priced into EA's 5m ton export increase; another 5-6m tons possible at the right price
- **Europe limited to ~5 GW German coal reserve** — months to bring online, maybe only peak demand (1 shift/day)

## Trading Implications

- **Bullish summer PJM sparks.** Even EA (who had a relatively bearish Q2 view per prior conversations) is now revising upward. The outage + congestion + coal price trinity is reinforcing.
- **Coal price risk is an upside tail.** US inventories low, Indonesia cuts tightening the global market. If Asian demand pulls more Atlantic coal, Eastern US coal prices rise → higher PJM marginal costs → sparks widen.
- **Graceton sticky until July changes the congestion timeline.** Our daily data shows Graceton returning briefly then going back out 3/23. If EA sees this persisting to July, the N-S congestion premium is structural, not episodic. This affects the entire Q2 congestion view.
- **Outage season thesis confirmed by EA.** Our data shows 46.6 GW and climbing. EA sees this as a multi-month bullish factor, not a weekly fluctuation.
- **Energy component of LMP elevated vs load** — this validates our observation that DARTs have been persistently positive. The stack is tighter than load alone suggests due to outages.

### Follow-Up: US Coal Export Capacity Constraints (3/20 — Mike Lawn)

Edi shared market chatter suggesting US coal export capacity is running near limits (~90% utilization implied). Mike confirms:

- **PRB (Powder River Basin) coal is essentially unexportable** — no Pacific NW port, Vancouver sold out for Canadian coal, tiny volumes possible via Mobile/CA
- **US export ceiling ~55m tonnes/annum** (recent annual record). EA forecasts 45m tonnes this year → **~10m tonnes of headroom** (~18% spare capacity)
- This tracks with the "90% utilization" chatter Edi referenced
- **Europeans will not sign long-term coal deals** — demand is structural decline, only short-term/spot interest
- Implication: the coal export headroom is real but limited. If Asia pulls hard enough to use that 10m tonne buffer, Eastern US coal prices reprice upward and flow through to PJM sparks
