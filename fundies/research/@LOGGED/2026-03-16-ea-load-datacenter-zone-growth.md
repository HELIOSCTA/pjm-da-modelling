# PJM Zone Load Growth — AEP & Dominion Dominate, Early Signs Elsewhere

## Summary

AEP and Dominion zones continue to dominate PJM load growth, with YTD y/y gains of 1.3 GW (7.5%) and 1.1 GW (6.8%) respectively. EA expects overall PJM weather-normalised load growth to reach 3% in 2026, including 0.6 GW of hyperscale data centres planned for Q1 26 that are expected to ramp faster than the default four-year timeline. ComEd and APS zones are showing early signs of growth but remain small in energy terms.

## Key Points

- **AEP**: +1.3 GW y/y (7.5%) YTD — largest contributor to PJM load growth
- **Dominion**: +1.1 GW y/y (6.8%) YTD — second largest contributor
- **Overall PJM**: Weather-normalised load growth expected to reach **3% in 2026**
- **Q1 26 data centres**: 0.6 GW of hyperscale connections planned; expected to ramp faster than EA's default four-year timeline
- **ComEd**: Growth doubled YTD vs 2025 in percentage point terms, but <0.2 MW in energy terms; Exelon requested 0.9 GW of large load adjustments to PJM's 2026 summer peak forecast, nearly all accepted
- **ComEd data centres**: ~0.4 GW of data centre capacity entering service this year, but far less will materialise as load
- **APS**: Growth up YTD but also small in energy terms

## Trading Implications

- **AEP/Dominion congestion thesis reinforced** — load growth concentrated in these zones supports structural congestion increase
- **ComEd worth watching** — 0.9 GW of accepted large load adjustments signals PJM is incorporating higher ComEd load into planning; could shift congestion patterns over time
- **3% weather-normalised growth is significant** — well above historical PJM trend; supports higher fossil call and summer spark pricing
- **Q1 26 data centre ramp risk** — faster-than-default ramp could surprise to the upside on near-term load
