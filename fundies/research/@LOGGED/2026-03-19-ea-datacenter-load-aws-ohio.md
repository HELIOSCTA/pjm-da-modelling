# AWS Jeffersonville Data Centre Campus — 0.5 GW Breaking Ground in DAY Zone

## Summary

Another large-scale AWS data centre campus has broken ground in the DAY zone southwest of Columbus, OH. The Jeffersonville campus is contracted for 0.5 GW of on-grid capacity, with potential to grow to 1.5 GW at full build-out. The site sits in the Columbus data centre load pocket that has driven much of the 1.1 GW y/y weather-normal AEP load growth through end-February. The remaining ~1 GW beyond on-grid is expected to be served largely by the nearby behind-the-meter Bluegrass Power plant (0.8 GW, DAY zone), which received Ohio Power Siting Board permits on 19 February.

## Key Points

- **AWS Jeffersonville campus**: 0.5 GW contracted on-grid capacity, up to 1.5 GW at full build-out
- **Location**: DAY zone, southwest of Columbus, OH — within the existing AEP data centre load pocket
- **Timeline**: +0.15 GW to DAY zone load by late 2026, +0.5 GW by end-2027
- **Behind-the-meter**: ~1 GW of remaining capacity expected to be served by **Bluegrass Power plant** (DAY zone, 0.8 GW), permitted by Ohio Power Siting Board on 19 February
- **Construction confirmed**: Satellite data indicates the site is under construction
- **AEP context**: Columbus pocket has been the primary driver of the 1.1 GW y/y weather-normal AEP load growth seen through end-February

## Trading Implications

- **AEP/DAY congestion thesis extended** — incremental 0.5 GW on-grid load in an already tight corridor; supports structural congestion increase in DAY zone
- **Near-term load ramp** — 0.15 GW by late 2026 is modest but adds to the cumulative ramp from other Q1 26 hyperscale connections (0.6 GW per EA's earlier estimates)
- **Behind-the-meter risk** — 0.8 GW Bluegrass plant serving ~1 GW BTM means this load largely won't show up in PJM metered demand or capacity market, but will affect local gas demand and transmission patterns
- **Capacity implications** — on-grid portion (0.5 GW) will need to clear in PJM capacity auctions; further tightens the AEP CONE picture
