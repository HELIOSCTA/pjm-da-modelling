# Energy Aspects — Power & Gas Weekly Outlook (Week of 16 March 2026)

## Summary

EA raised their peak-summer PJM-W NQ on-peak spark spread forecast to $49.18/MWh, driven by higher model load expectations, rising coal price risk, and congestion adding premium to West Hub. The weather-normal fossil call was recalibrated upward from 73.2 GW to 73.7 GW, now exceeding last year's realisation. PJM's long-term outage report shows record-high total outages for the balance of spring, adding further upside risk to spark spreads. On the gas side, end-October storage loosened 44 bcf w/w to 3.88 tcf as near-term weather moderates and L48 production trends higher. Waha faces further downside on spring Permian pipeline maintenance.

## Key Points

### PJM Summer Sparks (p.2)
- **PJM-W NQ spark forecast raised to $49.18/MWh** — higher model load, rising coal price risk, congestion premium to West Hub
- **NQ 2026 spark spreads outrunning gas prices** on war-driven jitters; NQ 2026 trading well above NQ 2023-2025 paths at equivalent days-to-expiry
- **Peak-summer fossil call recalibrated: 73.2 → 73.7 GW** — driven by +2.7 GW load growth, partially offset by -1.4 GW normal weather adjustment, -0.6 GW solar, -0.3 GW wind, -0.2 GW offshore wind
- Nuclear -0.0 GW (essentially flat y/y in NQ), hydro +0.1 GW

### Fuel Mix & Coal (p.3)
- **Diesel curve gains isolated risk** for PJM summer; ISO data likely overstates oil generation — EA adjusts using EIA data by fuel type
- **PJM coal inventories** likely to show incremental draws in Jan-Feb given Winter Storm Fern; NAPP coal prices have not responded despite higher y/y production
- **Coal capacity-adjusted inventories** tracking near 2025 levels (~0.4 Mt/GW in March), within five-year range

### Spring Outages (p.4, p.17)
- **PJM long-term outage forecast is record-high for spring 2026** — actual outages typically 18 GW above forecast over 90 days
- **EA outage forecast higher y/y** but not to PJM's extent; fossil outages up +1.3 GW y/y — clear upside risk to sparks
- Nuclear outages down -0.4 GW y/y in EA forecast
- **Hope Creek unplanned outage** lifted outages higher MTD y/y
- M3 forwards currently put most gas in merit against coal this spring despite slight NAPP rise

### Storage & EIA (p.5)
- **Thursday EIA estimate: +43 bcf injection** on warmer-than-normal temps triggering 9.9 bcf/d w/w decline in res-com demand
- **End-October storage loosened 44 bcf w/w to 3.88 tcf** as near-term weather moderates and L48 production trends higher
- Storage trajectory tracking near five-year average, above 2022 levels

### Production (p.6-7)
- **L48 dry gas: 111.6 bcf/d WTD** average, -0.2 bcf/d w/w on Appalachian freeze-offs
- **Appalachia: 33.0 bcf/d WTD**, -0.5 bcf/d w/w — regional lows to 15°F triggered freeze-offs; NEPA flows fell 0.2 bcf/d w/w
- **Permian: 23.0 bcf/d WTD**, -0.4 bcf/d w/w — March baseline expected to decline on updated spring maintenance schedule
- **Haynesville: 12.9 bcf/d WTD**, flat w/w — rigs continue to defy expectations, supporting Q4 26 production growth estimate

### Trade (p.8)
- **Canada imports: 4.9 bcf/d WTD**, +1.9 bcf/d higher w/w on weather-driven demand and decline in Canadian exports
- **Mexico exports: 6.4 bcf/d WTD**, -0.5 bcf/d w/w — power outages in Mexico drove gas demand down, especially past Brownsville

### Demand (p.9)
- **Power gas demand for week ending 3/20: 29.7 bcf/d**, -1.4 bcf/d w/w
- **Res-com demand**: late-season cold shot pushed HDD estimates for week ending 3/20 to realise on par with 10-year normal, triggering +10.4 bcf/d increase in res-com

### LNG (p.10)
- **LNG feedgas: 19.2 bcf/d WTD**, +0.9 bcf/d w/w — Freeport +0.5, Sabine Pass +0.4
- **Sabine Pass: 5.2 bcf/d WTD** with improvement in utilisation; Creole Trail pipeline maintenance (0.83 bcf/d impact) through 19 March

### Industrial (p.11)
- **Steel capacity utilisation**: fell 0.7 ppts w/w to 76.7%
- **Refinery utilisation**: crude imports via Strait of Hormuz account for just 3% of USGC refinery runs; greater access to Latin America heavies limits disruption risk

### Positioning & Rigs (p.12)
- **Henry Hub net positioning: +136.3k lots net long**, +25.7k lots w/w — lengthening in sympathy with crude and LNG prices
- **Total rigs (13 Mar): 553** (+2 w/w, -40 y/y); gas rigs 133 (+1 w/w, +31 y/y); oil rigs 412 (+1 w/w, -74 y/y)
- **Haynesville rigs: 54** (+1 w/w, +25 y/y) — sustained additions since Winter Storm Fern
- Oil producers have not responded to high crude prices with more rigs

### Waha & Permian Egress (p.13)
- **Waha delivered cash only positive during Winter Storm Fern this winter**
- **EA models -$1.56/MMBtu average Mar-May 2026** before new egress lifts prices
- Permian pipelines beginning spring maintenance; largest outages yet to be scheduled
- El Paso and Northern Natural experiencing long-term force majeures

### WCSB Production (p.14)
- **WCSB producers guiding 4.8% production growth y/y** on CAD$21.9B capex, though expectations tempered since Q3 25
- **EA forecasts WCSB at 21.5 bcf/d in 2026**, +0.9 bcf/d y/y, limited by storage capacity
- Tourmaline revised down from 3,126 to 2,866 mmcf/d; CNRL unchanged at 2,669

### Load Growth (p.15)
- **PJM weather-normalised load growth: 2.9% for 2026**, 3.2% for 2027
- **PJM summer peak load: 166.6 GW (2026)**, +5.2% y/y; winter peak 145.6 GW, +1.4% y/y
- Winter peak load expected to grow faster than summer in PJM due to **heating electrification and BTM solar**
- Load growth expectations raised outside PJM and ERCOT to align with vendor data

### Coal (p.16)
- **NAPP prices have not risen outside the war rally** despite low PJM stocks
- Export data likely to be revised higher but does not yet show uptick despite rising Asian demand
- **Flat-to-slightly-lower production y/y** forecast as generator inventories near target levels through summer under normal weather

### Gas Generation (p.18)
- **Gas share of thermal generation above 70%** through shoulder season — warm weather + cheaper gas prices
- **April gas generation expected +5 GW y/y** as prompt HH remains $1/MMBtu lower y/y
- **Coal generation expected -6 GW lower y/y in April** on lower HH prices

### Hydro (p.19)
- US West precipitation near 20-year average, driving reservoir replenishment
- Ultimate generation levels uncertain as water-to-snow ratio is unprecedented

## Trading Implications

- **Long summer sparks supported** — $49.18 forecast with upside risk from record spring outages and rising coal costs
- **Fossil call at 73.7 GW exceeds 2025 realisation** — load growth is structural, not just weather
- **Spring outage season is the key risk** — PJM's record-high outage forecast means available capacity could tighten sharply through May
- **Storage loosening is bearish near-term gas** — 3.88 tcf end-Oct estimate moderating; +43 bcf injection expected Thursday
- **Waha deeply negative through May** — short Waha basis or avoid long Permian-linked positions through maintenance season
- **NAPP coal non-responsive** — if coal prices eventually catch up to tighter stocks, could lift dark spreads and support sparks further
- **Winter peak load growing faster than summer in PJM** — heating electrification + BTM solar structurally shifting the load shape
