# Energy Aspects — PJM Record-High Spring Outage Forecast (March 2026)

## Summary

PJM's 90-day long-term outage forecast is projecting very high spring generation outages — 4 GW higher y/y than last year's forecast on this date. Historical patterns show actual outages typically realise 15-20 GW above the mid-March long-term forecast through mid-May, and 20-25 GW above from mid-May through June, as unplanned outages materialise. This suggests actual outages could exceed 70 GW at peak outage season in late April.

EA's model forecasts nuclear outages down 0.4 GW y/y and fossil fuel outages up 1.3 GW y/y for spring, but is below the PJM long-term forecast after accounting for unplanned outages.

Outages over the last two weeks have already averaged 4.0 GW higher y/y, with nuclear outages at Calvert Cliffs 1 (BGE, 0.9 GW) and Hope Creek (AE, 1.2 GW) contributing 0.3 GW of the y/y increase.

## Key Points

- **PJM 90-day outage forecast is 4 GW above last year** on the same date — record-high for spring
- **Actual outages typically exceed the mid-March forecast by 15-25 GW** through the spring maintenance season (mid-April through June)
- **Peak outage season (late April) could see 70+ GW offline** — compared to current ~42.6 GW
- **Nuclear outages:** Calvert Cliffs 1 (0.9 GW, BGE — now returned as of 3/17) and Hope Creek (1.2 GW, AE) — 0.3 GW of the y/y increase
- **Fossil fuel outages up 1.3 GW y/y** in EA's model — planned maintenance window expanding
- **West Hub realised spark spreads: $35.75/MWh** over last 2 weeks, **+$12.90 y/y** despite gas prices $1.08/MMBtu lower — outages + load growth + Conastone congestion driving spreads
- **Conastone transmission outages** specifically called out as a driver of elevated West Hub spreads

## Trading Implications

- **Structurally bullish for PJM through May-June.** If outages ramp from current 42.6 GW toward 70 GW by late April, reserve margins tighten significantly. The evening ramp blowouts we've seen this week (3/12 $889, 3/17 $129 HE20) could become more frequent as fewer units are available.
- **West Hub spark spreads already elevated** — $35.75/MWh with $1.08 lower gas means the spread is being driven entirely by power price strength from outages + congestion. If outages increase further, spreads could widen.
- **The 15-20 GW historical forecast-to-actual uplift** is critical context for our outage queries (06/06b). Current 42.6 GW + 15-20 GW unplanned = 57-63 GW by mid-May. At 70 GW in late April, the capacity margin on a 120 GW peak day would be extremely tight.
- **Calvert Cliffs 1 return (3/17) is a near-term bearish offset** — removes 0.9 GW from the outage stack. But Hope Creek (1.2 GW) is still out.
- **Reinforces the operator effect thesis:** As outages climb toward 70 GW, operator tools become more constrained → DA congestion becomes more accurate → the positive DART on congestion should narrow.
