# Energy Aspects — PJM Reserve Reforms Would Be Moderately Bullish to LMPs If Approved (2026-03-20)

## Summary

EA analysis of the PJM Reserve Certainty Senior Task Force (RCSTF) proposal from 3/17/2026 to dramatically overhaul reserve markets. The proposal retires Primary Reserve (PR) and replaces it with new Ramp/Uncertainty Reserve (RUR) products — 10-minute RUR up/down and 30-minute RUR up — aimed at addressing increasing net-load ramping uncertainty from renewable forecast error. The new products would be partially unnested from Synchronized Reserves, creating new ORDCs similar to ERCOT's RTC+B design.

PJM simulated the new requirements against 2025 data. Results show moderate LMP uplift concentrated on high net-load days in summer and winter, with minimal impact in spring/autumn.

**Timeline:** June vote → MRC August → Members Committee September → FERC filing October → earliest go-live January 2027. But stakeholder approval is far from certain — the RCSTF's previous DA 30-min reserve proposal failed at MRC in 2024.

## Key Points

- **New reserve products:** 10-min RUR up, 10-min RUR down, 30-min RUR up replace Primary Reserve. 30-min RUR nests within existing Total 30-min; SR, 10-min RUR, and 30-min RUR unnest from each other
- **Energy price cap rises from $3,700/MWh to $4,100/MWh** — SR ORDC cap increases to $2,100/MWh; when added to $2,000/MWh hard energy offer cap, system prices can reach $4,100 (+$400 vs current). Congestion/losses on top
- **Summer DA LMP uplift: avg +$5/MWh ATC** on representative high-load days. Peak uplift +$6.14/MWh on 24 June simulation. But that day's DA ATC was ~$170, so only a 3% increase
- **Winter uplift: +$8/MWh.** Spring/autumn: slight reduction in LMPs (lower net loads)
- **+0.7 GW more reserves procured on-peak in summer** vs 2025 actuals. Largest increase HE10-14 at +1.7 GW (30-min RUR product) — this is peak solar ramp uncertainty
- **HE17-19 super-peak: flat to slightly higher reserves but DA LMPs -$14/MWh** — counterintuitive. Likely because 30-min RUR requirement declines at peak-load hour (less ramp uncertainty), and smooth ORDCs replace existing step curves
- **Day-Ahead Scheduling Reserve (DASR):** New product to bring operator out-of-market commitments into market (similar to ISONE DASI). Secondary reserves capped at $50/MWh, clear at $0 outside emergencies
- **ORDCs modeled on ERCOT RTC+B** — allow PJM to go short reserves if least-cost. Hierarchy: SR most valuable → 10-min RUR → 30-min RUR → 30-min reserve

## Trading Implications

- **Not yet tradeable** — EA is NOT raising 2027 spark forecasts due to approval uncertainty and concentrated impact on already-elevated days. But this is a known upside risk to track
- **Biggest impact during solar hours (HE10-14)** — +1.7 GW reserve procurement when net-load ramping steepest. As PJM solar capacity grows, this effect amplifies. Structural bullish for shoulder-month solar ramp hours
- **Higher price ceiling ($4,100 vs $3,700)** matters for tail risk pricing. On scarcity days, the maximum system price is $400/MWh higher. This affects vol and the distribution of extreme outcomes
- **HE17-19 LMP decline is surprising** — if the reform actually lowers super-peak prices by $14/MWh while raising solar-ramp prices, it flattens the intraday shape. Bears monitoring for shape-trade implications
- **Prior RCSTF failure at MRC in 2024** is the key risk. Complexity of the proposal + stakeholder opposition could delay or kill it. June vote is the first gate
