# Energy Aspects — Virginia RGGI Rejoin Unlikely by July 2026 (2026-04-02)

## Summary

Energy Aspects views Virginia's return to RGGI by July 2026 as unlikely despite the DEQ's stated timeline, maintaining their PJM-W NQ26 spark forecast unchanged. Their emissions team sees a 2027 rejoin date as more likely.

## Key Points

- Virginia DEQ announced intent to rejoin RGGI by July 2026 and participate in September and December auctions
- EA emissions team sees 2027 rejoin as more likely — no change to PJM-W NQ26 spark forecast
- Current RGGI allowance replacement cost: **$28.79/st**
- Virginia fossil plant dispatch costs would rise by **~$17/MWh** assuming average emissions rate weighted by state fossil fleet technology mix
- Higher VA dispatch costs would incentivise increased imports from non-RGGI states, **lifting RTO-wide energy component of LMP**
- DOM congestion will continue to rise as bulk power transmission line utilisation grows
- Western zones would see **negative congestion** when such constraints bind (i.e., cheaper western generation gets bottlenecked trying to serve DOM load)

## Trading Implications

- **NQ26 sparks**: No change — EA doesn't expect Virginia RGGI costs to hit the market until 2027
- **DOM congestion**: Structurally bullish as transmission utilisation grows regardless of RGGI timeline; RGGI rejoin would amplify this
- **Western zone basis**: Risk of negative congestion in AEP/western PJM when east-flowing constraints bind — watch for this as a spread opportunity
- **RTO LMP**: Mild upside when RGGI does take effect — $17/MWh cost adder on VA fossil fleet shifts the merit order
