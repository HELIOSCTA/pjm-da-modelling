#### (EA) Regulatory risk a significant headwind for PJM and ERCOT data-centre projects [https://my.energyaspects.com/live?postUid=blt06bccd189faa75b8]
See: [[PJM/LOGGED/co-located]]

PJM’s initial FERC filing to amend its co-location rules could encourage data centres to co-locate with existing capacity, or else submit planned generating capacity into PJM’s interconnection queue. Large data centres have increasingly looked to islanded solutions to circumvent interconnection queue delays, but PJM's filing could present challenges to that strategy.  
  
Existing or planned generators larger than 50 MW serving load behind-the-meter must sign an interconnection agreement with PJM under the proposal, which would classify such resources as a Co-Located Generating Facility. Load in a co-located configuration would be able to net the capacity value of the generating resource against transmission and other grid costs. The proposal would also establish the different transmission services that co-located loads could receive, although service details were not included in this filing.  
  
Over 55% of data centres currently under construction in PJM are larger than 50 MW, with that share rising to nearly 75% for planned projects.
