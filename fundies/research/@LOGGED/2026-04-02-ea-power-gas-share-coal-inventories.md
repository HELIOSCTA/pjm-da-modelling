# Energy Aspects — PJM Gas Share Rising, Aiding Coal Inventories (2026-04-02)

## Summary

PJM gas share has risen 1.3 ppts week-over-week as mild shoulder season weather, higher outages, and lower delivered gas prices allow coal inventories to recover after exiting winter below generator target levels. Coal generation fell over 1 GW (12%) in the last week on a 4.4 GW lower fossil call w/w.

## Key Points

- PJM gas share up **+1.3 ppts w/w** — mild weather, higher outages, low delivered gas prices
- Coal generation down **>1 GW (12%)** from MTD levels on **4.4 GW lower fossil call w/w**
- Delivered M3 prices down **$0.30/MMBtu w/w**, supporting gas share
- PJM coal inventories declined **11% in January** after entering winter already below target levels of **60 days' forward supply**
- EA estimates generators burned **13.3 Mt of coal** over peak winter — slightly lower y/y but in line with previous colder-than-normal winters
- NAPP physical prices rising on **global coal balance tightening**, not PJM inventory draws — suggests generators are sufficiently contracted bal-year

## Trading Implications

- **Shoulder season coal relief**: Lower fossil call gives coal inventories room to rebuild — reduces the tail risk of coal supply tightness into summer
- **Spark vs dark spreads**: Rising gas share means gas is on the margin more often — sparks more relevant than darks in the current shoulder environment
- **Summer risk**: If coal inventories don't sufficiently rebuild during shoulder season, summer heat could re-expose the supply tightness. Monitor EIA weekly coal stock reports
- **NAPP price signal**: Global-driven rather than PJM-driven coal price increases — less of a direct dispatch cost concern if generators are hedged bal-year
