Don't sell DA when low renewables overlap with outage season
- March 26th: sold DA at $48 and $50.50, DA cleared $54.54. Low solar forecast for tomorrow combined with strong outages left very few ramping units in PEPCO. Outages remove baseload capacity; low renewables remove the flexible generation that compensates. Together they compound — the stack gets structurally tight and DA clears well above where recent prints suggest fair value. Before selling DA in spring outage season, check the renewables forecast. If solar is low and outages are elevated, the supply cushion is gone and you're selling into a tight stack with asymmetric upside risk.
- Pay attention to P75 and P90 of the like day model — if those upside scenarios are well above your sell price, the risk/reward is telling you not to sell.

Never sell RT between 3-4pm to pick up $1-2 when it can go in your face
- March 24th first trading day: sold at $42.75 and it settled $50. Gave up $7+ chasing $1-2 of edge. The 3-4pm window is when the evening ramp sets up — tail risk is massively asymmetric to the upside. If you're short RT going into HE19-20, you're selling a cheap option on the ramp.

Monitor which key ramping and baseload units are coming off — outages set the gap higher quickly
- March 26th: no ramping units left in PEPCO. When baseload units trip or go on planned outage during spring outage season, the remaining fleet has no flexibility to absorb load swings. The gap between available supply and demand widens fast, and DA/RT both reprice higher.
- Check the outage map daily — know which units in your zone are down and what role they play (baseload vs. ramping vs. peaker). If the ramping units are gone, the stack is brittle.

Learn when to step in and out of intraday trades
- Morning ramp: close or reduce when Bath County starts pumping — that's the signal the ramp is being managed.
- Midday bearish valley: close the trade during the solar-driven selloff rather than holding through dead hours hoping for evening.
- Evening ramp: if the print comes, take it. Don't hold through the spike waiting for more — the blowout is the exit, not the entry to a bigger position.

Soft holidays (Good Friday, Easter) suppress load — don't buy DA into them
- April 2nd 2026: bought DA at $50 x 150 MWh, DA cleared $47.50. Good Friday demand consistently underperforms PJM's forecast — at least 4% below over the morning peak for the last 8 years, often 5%+ (nearly 8% in 2019). Easter Sunday also underperforms but less so since weekend suppression is already baked in. The holiday isn't a bank holiday so the load miss isn't obvious, but travel and closures still drag demand. Before buying DA ahead of soft holidays, haircut the load forecast — PJM's model doesn't fully capture the behavioural demand drop.
